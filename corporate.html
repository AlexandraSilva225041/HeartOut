Production

Design Elements

  • A colour scheme
  • #1E1E1E Black is the first thing that comes to mind when we think of Hard Rock. It represents the darkness, intense feelings, extremes, and aesthetic value of the music genre. It's edgy and even scary. #1E1E1E is a slightly lighter black, making it easier to contrast.
  • #2E0038 Dark Purple goes along side the black on this one, as it is a powerful color frequently used in album covers and other rock artwork. It is dynamic and slightly unexpected.
  • #AE0909 The main value of HeartOut is Passion, and that is what this burning red represents. It is also heavily used in rock artwork.
  • #49662C Looking at album covers and different artworks, hard rock can be quite colourful. It was decided to add this dim green, symbolizing safety, nature, humanity.
  • #FF8000 This saturated yellow brings some contrast to the color scheme, differentiating it. It is also a great symbol of power, Energy and Community.
  • Font choices

    • Medieval Sharp - Header Font

    Medieval Sharp is a, as the name says, sharp and powerful font. It embodies some curves while keeping its energy and harshness. It is still readable. Various hard rock artoworks, such as album covers, include similar, medieval style fonts. This typeface is suitable for headers as it calls attention and adds personality to the brand.


    Roboto Regular - Body Type

    Roboto is a simple and readable typeface. Since the website has a dark background, which makes it more difficult to create contrast, as well as a considerable amount of text and detail, readability is very important.


    Justification of Navigation and UI patterns

    In the Home Page, the visitor gets an understanding of what they can do on the website, in other words, of the product, and what is the meaning of their actions. The power they hold by being able to help other people by writing a review, as well as checking other reviews. The second one is more encouraged (through a button) since it is a smoother way to engage witht the community at first. Since the beginning, it should be obvious for the visitor that HeartOut is not like most online community where people can share their interest, but rather a place where they can be helpful for each other, which connects to the USP. The containers help separate the copy in the page and the video-replayer makes sit more dynamic. (PUT VALUES IN THE COPY OF THIS PAGE)


    In the About Us page, the actual values of the brand are stated, and a better explanation of the brand, rather than product. It is more about the main idea of HeartOut. The separation of the product and brand has the intention of intensifying the idea of each one, instead of mixing them. The product may be the reviews website, but the brand is about the community and helping each other. There is also an introduction to the concept development team, bringing the visitors closer to the where the concept came from. It includes pictures of the members since, according to research, hard rock fans like face to face interactions and enjoy closeness. This page uses simple containers.


    The reviews are in a whole separate page, as it makes sense for them to be all together in one place. The cards create contrast between each event. By clicking the review the visitor can see it more in detail on a separate page, which helps focusing on one event at a time. Columns are used in the reviews part of the website, since it was the best way to divide the categories and the stars reviews while being aligned. Hard rock fans are often interested in specific artist or venues, so they can view the specific review categories only on those events. The button “Write Review” leads to a separate page where the visitor can focus only on this.


    According to the research conducted, hard rock fans do not like to chat online, and the communication works more on the basis of word of mouth. That is why this rating system works. They do not have to text or comment, but have that possibility. This way they are sure that the event they intent on going will match their expectations.


    The category ratings such as Performance, Vibes, Safety, Offline and Hard Hard Rock connect with the brand values of Passion, Loyalty, Safety, and Feeling Present in the Moment. Research showed that hard rock fans tend to not be on their phones during concerts and care a lot about the loud music while keeping its quality.

    Testing Report

    • The important factors of the test were focused on navigating through the website and the look and feel of the brand. We do this to confirm that vision and values come across well by our users. From the result, we can make improvements in our mobile and laptop versions of the website.
    • For the testing, we used four participants. We split the participants, and with two participants we focused on the mobile version of the website. For the other two, we focused on the laptop version in this way we can test in multiple ways the accessibility of the website. We ask the participants the following questions:
    • Read one of the reviews?
    • Submit a review.
    • Do you feel like the values of the are portrayed on the website?
    • How do you feel about the colors?
    • How does navigation make you feel?
    • Are there any other improvements?
    • The overall opinion was positive, the participants found it easy to navigate and were satisfied with the constituency of the website in the mobile and laptop versions. However, a participant from the laptop group was distracted by the moving visual behind the review button therefore he was not sure where to click. In the mobile group, the participant found the website readable however the review page, the reviews were too big as a result he only saw one review per scroll. He suggested making them smaller so it is more of an overview look. There were different opinions about the values some agreed some were not.
    • From these outcomes, we can improve the website by working on experiencing are vision and values on the website and making navigating through the website even easier.

    Marketing

    Context of campaign and promotional activities

    HeartOut is an online community for hard rock fans in Noord Brabrant, where all kinds of people can connect over enjoying music that puts their hearts on fire. On the website, users can find, read, and write reviews of past concerts or events, making it easier for them to choose venues, bands, and facilitate their overall experience. HeartOut makes sure that the hard rock fans under their wing match absolutely the vibe of the event they are attending. This brand supports passion and feeling in the moment, as well as loyalty for bands and assuring safety in different experiences. Hard Rock fans are there for each other, and we are there for them.


    Message – There is a new online community that calls hard rock fans of all ages and different lifestyles to connect over what they value most: living in the moment with burning passion. Through the review system, they are there for each other, and we are here for them. Purpose – The purpose of the campaign is to create a reliable and loyal social media platform to encourage followers to use the HeartOut website. It is to hype/build up for the upcoming website but also a platform that keeps being alive, attracting and inspiring new followers all the time.

    Target Audience

    Demographic and Geographic Age – All ages; 5-95 years old (age is not a relevant point, people of all ages attend concerts, families go together many times) Nationality – Dutch and European internationals (more likely to be into the same bands and have an idea of the venues) Region – Noord Brabant, Netherlands Other – Somehow, hard rock helped them go through a difficult moment, for example, blow off steam during a phase of behavioural issues Psycographic Personal relations – close to family, especially parents. Prefer to have structure, plans need to be predictable. Dislike feeling out of place. Caring people to those around them. Cheerful and positive. Usually reserved people. Values – Cares about feeling like they belong to the environment, the “vibes” need to match with them. This applies not only to hard rock events but also anything in their lives. They care about enjoying an event or experience in peace, so the feeling of safety and security is important. They also believe that people should live more in the moment, and not succumb to routine. Experience strong emotions is a form of self-expression and of connecting to people. Need – A (geographically) close online community where they can connect and feel closer to the people by meeting them in concerts; Somehow predicting the vibe of the concert and if it fits them Solution to the need – a place to share valuable feedback in order to make an informed decision about going or not to a concert, in an uptight community that values the same categories Behavioural Social media habits – Follows accounts related to hard rock, like fan-pages or official bands, but does not interact with them, however it is enough to keep them informed of events. Also does not chat with friends online much. The persona developed with this information can be viewed in the appendix, through this link.


    Decision Making Process

    To go to a concert, the decision is taken by the individual, usually months before, rarely last minute, around friends or family The website might be used between friends, as they get to know it exists by word of mouth when they are together. The “vibe match” promise triggers them to use the website, and also curiosity since this type of online community is not common between hard rock fans


    Positioning

    HeartOut aims to grow a now underdeveloped online community, focused on the target audience segment that likes socializing and uses face-to-face communication. As mentioned in the decision making process, hard rock fans tend to feel more encouraged to take action in a physical group setting. Considering these two factors, it makes sense to understand where they meet physically. According to the interviews conducted, hard rock fans usually meet up with their friends at bars, where there are posters and flyers about upcoming events. HeartOut could spread posters feauturing the app around bars, with for example a monthly overview of events and brand review for one of the venues, for example. In this case, both the overview of events and review system are added benefits compared to competitors around. Hard rock fans also make use of the internet, not to interact among each other, but as a way to access information. On websites such as TicketMaster, TripAdvisor, and Locaties, an overview of events or venues can be found easily. On social media, hard rock fans often follow several pages with content about bands, the bands themselves, venues, and festivals. HeartOut is no different than competitors in that sense. However, once again, HeartOut offers the benefit of a non-biased, outside identity that has the added benefit of reviewing events through several categories.

    Learning Points

    We learned from our interviews that hard rock fans are not highly active on social media and interacting online with one and other. We set our goal to raise engagement by a 1000 people and wanted the response to be sharing word of mouth with their friends and to react with ahre.
    We quickly noticed when posting on social media we had somewhat of a reach but those we reached tended to not engage. We switched our tactic and posted a moving visual ´reel´ on Instagram and Facebook. After posting this we went from an average of 21 views to 60 views on Instagram. While we did expect an increase in views a surprising finding was that the reel did not get more likes than on some of our images. This could mean that the increase in views in the real could come from a broader audience by instagram algorithm boosting a reel with a trend. This would mean the target audience themselves tends to interact with images more than reels for us.
    The post with the famous‘ ‘angry music for happy people’-august burns red ‘ got most of the likes confirming one of our findings from the beginning that the hard rock fans prefer to engage with the specific content , bands, music, sayings they like and agree with rather than just images of hard rock. Lastly, our post about our values got the same amount of likes. We created our brand values based on the target audience’s values. We found out in our interviews that their values mean a lot to them and therefore them engaging with values they stand for was less surprising too. Any of our posts with jut a hard rock or brand image got less engagement.
    We can conclude from this that our target audience really seeks their values and loyal artist or bands in the content they engage with and that simply hard rock images do not interest them enough to engage with even when it reaches them. This means that for our future we should put deep thought and meaning behind every post made. Read for more details about this in our future planning below.
    Finally we realised during our project that we put more effort into instagram than facebook. This resulted in us having barely any likes at all on facebook. We should have divided the tactics for them better.


    Future Planning

    In our communication planning, our biggest learning point is not using the same strategy on two different platforms. We solve this by posting more frequently and diverse kinds of posts, so there is more variety. From our learning points we notify that are most liked post is something recognisable. For future post, we will create more of those kinds of posts. Since we are a profit-making company, we start to work with sponsorship to increase the revenue stream and create a bigger awareness among our target audience. In this way we can collaborate with other brands, therefore also use them on social media what will create a different variety of social media posts. In objective it is mentioned to raise awareness by word of mouth despite being difficult to track it is a key factor for our target audience. We will be focusing more on offline marketing, therefore hanging posters in local pubs, bars, and pop stages. In this way, Heart Out is trying to be closer to our main goal to share our passion for hard rock music with others.
